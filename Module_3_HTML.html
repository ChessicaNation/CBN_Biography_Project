Experience

Saturday Night Live (1988 - 1991)

  • Served as a writer for three seasons of the sketch comedy show.
  • Received an Emmy Award for Outstanding Writing for a Variety Series in 1989, with nominations in 1990 and 1991.

The Simpsons (1991 - 1993)

  • Served as a writer and producer for two seasons of the animated comedy sitcom.
  • Wrote some of the series' most acclaimed episodes, including "Marge vs. the Monorail" and "Homer Goes to College."

Late Night with Conan O'Brien (1993 - 2009)

  • Hosted sixteen seasons of the late-night talk show, with an average audience of 2.5 million viewers.
  • Received an Emmy Award for Best Writing in a Comedy or Variety Series in 2007, with annual nominations from 1996 - 2009.
  • Received Writers Guild of America Awards for Comedy/Variety (Including Talk) - Series in 1997, 2000, 2002, 2003, 2005, and 2006, with nominations in 1999, 2001, 2004, 2007, 2008, and 2009.

The Tonight Show with Conan O'Brien (2009 - 2010)

  • Hosted the late-night talk show for seven months.
  • Nominated for an Emmy Award for Outstanding Variety Series and Outstanding Writing for a Variety, Music, or Comedy Series in 2010.
  • Nominated for a Writers Guild of America Award for Comedy/Variety (Including Talk) - Series in 2010.

"The Legally Prohibited from Being Funny on Television Tour" (2010)

  • Headlined a two month, 30-city live tour throughout the United States and Canada.
  • Nearly every location sold out tickets.

Conan (2010 - 2021)

  • Hosted the late-night talk show for eleven seasons.
  • Became an international trailblazer by being the first American television personality in more than fifty years to film in Cuba, the first American late-night host to ever film a show from Armenia, and visiting North Korea on a technicality by stepping across the border at the Korean Demilitarized Zone. Other international filming locations included Qatar, Germany, Mexico, Israel, Haiti, Italy, Japan, Australia, Ghana, and Greenland.
  • Received an Emmy Award for Outstanding Interactive Program in 2012, with various Emmy Award Nominations in 2011, 2013, 2016, 2019, and 2021.
  • Nominated for Critics' Choice Awards Best Talk Show in 2012, 2013, and 2014.

Conan O'Brien Needs a Friend (2018 - Current)

  • Hosts weekly podcast with a new guest each episode. Notable guests include Barack and Michelle Obama, Hillary Clinton, Sonia Sotomayor, along with over a hundred other comedians, actors, and entertainers.
  • Won two iHeartRadio Podcast Awards in 2020, with a third nomination in the same year.
  • The podcast reached over 250 million downloads as of August 2021 and averages over 9 million downloads per month.
